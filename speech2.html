Informative Speech Reflection

My speech, Robots - Sci-fi or Reality?, was a speech on the recent shift towards humanoid robots. It taught the audience of the concerns around robots, as well as their benefits. New AI technology greatly supported such a new shift, as well as the benefit of having a robot do the mundane, repetitive and unsafe tasks of humans.

My choice of topic, humanoid robots, is a decent topic. I say this because many new robots, such as Boston Dynamic's Atlas, have been released or are planned to start releasing in late 2026 and 2027, which is relatively soon. For that reason I believed it has good kairos, and I wanted to teach everybody what people have talked about around these robots. What I think may have caused it to not be the best topic is that not many people in the audience are too interested in these robots, as their existence doesn't really affect them, nor will until a few years.

My attention getter was creating a slightly humorous scene of entering a store, looking for a jar of pickles, and finding out that the employee was actually a robot. Essentially a hypothetical situation.

I did well in starting out my speech with my attention getter, and then returning back to it as my clincher at the conclusion for a nice way to wrap up the speech. My annunciation was clear, and my volume was loud; I had made sure I was heard well. I had pretty good eye contact with the audience, and I felt like I was speaking to them rather than at them.

I had practiced my attention getter multiple times, and I knew I wanted something a little humorous, and I knew my audience, and I knew I would get a slight chuckle if the "product" was a jar of pickles. As for my annunciation, I had achieved that by rehearsing multiple times, and also looking at myself when I rehearsed. I made sure that I am able to speak clearly, as I wanted to try my best not to stutter. During my first speech, I kept looking up, and I tried my best to fix it this time around, and I feel like I did a better job at looking at the audience.

I struggled with using signposts. I had a lot of difficulty transitioning, especially with saying my sources. I have gotten a little bit better with decreasing filler words, but my speech flow is still choppy, especially in the second half where I had this huge pause. I also got ahead of myself in the first point, even saying "let me backtrack a little", which would never happen in a regular speech.

I think what led to why I wasn't good with signposts was the way I remembered the concepts I wanted to talk about. I remembered them as "bullets" so those transitions didn't exist in my head. I know to make note of them next time. Getting better with signposts should also help my speech flow, and I should watch out from saying "uh". Finally, I need to make sure I organize my speech a bit better on the outline. I bulleted the details, but I did not put much thought into what should go first, rather than later. That messed me up during the speech.

First, I got my speech thought out in my head. Then I wrote an outline based off of the information I found and how I wanted to talk about it. For rehearsing I rehearsed in front of peers at least once each day I came to school, so I was able to rehearse four times in front of people. I rehearsed in front of Colden and Raghad mainly, with Dylan, Emma, and Sam in some other occasions. I also rehearsed by myself, and I recorded myself doing that three times. Thankfully, I completed my outline rather quickly, and I had lots of time to rehearse. I do feel much more confident compared to my intro speech, and words flowed better. There is still room for improvement, but I definitely feel way better. This can easily be seen just in the amount of filler words I said in this speech(only three) compared to the last speech, which had more than seven.

My goal was to try my best to do a good conclusion, and to maintain eye contact. I was able to achieve these goals, as I practiced those especially just to make sure I don't mess them up like I did during the introductory speech.

In the next speech my goal is to have a better use of signposts. I want to be able to transition smoothly from one point to another, since I struggled with that during this speech.
